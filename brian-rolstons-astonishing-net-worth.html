Biography of Brian Rolston

Retired American professional ice hockey player Brian Lee Rolston, born on February 21, 1973, is best known for his time with the Boston Bruins in the National Hockey League. His accolades include a Stanley Cup championship with the New Jersey Devils in 1995, and a triumph in the World Cup of Hockey in 1996 as part of Team USA. He has also represented the United States in three Olympic ice hockey competitions, winning a Silver Medal in the Salt Lake City Olympics of 2002. Rolston hails from Flint, Michigan, and was raised in Ann Arbor, Michigan. He is the proud father of four sons: Ryder Scott, Brody, Stone, and Zane. Notably, his elder brother is Ron Rolston, the former coach of the Buffalo Sabres. Rolston calls Traverse City, Michigan, home.
