BTS And Fifty Fifty Are The Only Two K-pop Groups To Make It To One Of Billboard's Radio Charts

Just months into their journey, Fifty Fifty has achieved a groundbreaking milestone with their breakout hit, “Cupid.” The infectious track rapidly climbs global charts and debuted on the prestigious Billboard ranking.

This accomplishment not only places Fifty Fifty among the most successful acts in the industry but also establishes them as trailblazers within the South Korean music scene.

Fifty Fifty’s Entry onto the Adult Pop Airplay Chart: Debuting at No. 39 on this week’s Adult Pop Airplay chart, “Cupid” marks Fifty Fifty’s presence on a chart that measures the popularity of songs on adult pop radio stations across the United States.

While it may be a lower-ranking debut, the significance lies in Fifty Fifty is only the second K-pop act to achieve this feat.

Following in BTS’s Footsteps: Now that Fifty Fifty has joined BTS on the Adult Pop Airplay list, they have cemented their position in music history.

In 2020, BTS made waves by becoming the first South Korean entity to chart on this prestigious radio platform with their smash hit “Dynamite,” reaching an impressive No. 10. The group’s subsequent release, “Butter,” peaked at No. 14 in 2021, further demonstrating their crossover appeal.

Collaborative Success: BTS continued their chart domination on the Adult Pop Airplay chart by teaming up with pop rockers Coldplay for the collaborative track “My Universe.”

The song soared to No. 8, securing the highest placement achieved by a South Korean musical act on the chart. This achievement exemplifies the global impact and versatility of BTS’s music.

Fifty Fifty’s Historic Achievement: With its debut on the Adult Pop Airplay chart, Fifty Fifty cements its position alongside BTS as a groundbreaking South Korean music act.

Their achievement highlights the global recognition of K-pop and signifies the increasing acceptance and appreciation of diverse music styles in the Western market.

Conclusion: Fifty Fifty’s breakout hit “Cupid” has made history by debuting on the Adult Pop Airplay chart, joining the ranks of BTS as the second South Korean music act to achieve this feat.

Their accomplishment further showcases the growing influence and success of K-pop on a global scale, breaking down barriers and paving the way for future acts to make their mark in the Western music industry.
