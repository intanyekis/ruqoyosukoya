Caviar Releases IPhone 12 Editions Honoring Jeff Bezos &amp; Elon Musk

Long are the days where a colorful phone case would suffice to bring a little touch of originality and a feel of luxury to our devices. People have now high expectations, and high-tech companies continuously find ways to deliver everyone's demand for new creations, and this company particularly managed well to cater to our fantasy iPhone design!

Via LetsGoDigital

A customized line of Apple's iPhone 12 Pro Max has been launched by Caviar, a luxury brand. The high-class design company is especially known for its limited edition of smartphones, as well as other devices such as video game consoles.

Caviar's line was launched in order to celebrate the 60th anniversary of the first-ever manned spaceflight which happened in 1961. As a way to commemorate this historical accomplishment, the luxury brand has named their new iPhone's limited edition designs, "Space Conquerors".

Furthermore, the brand is famous for expertly craft and create limited quantities of state-of-the-art high-tech designs. Therefore, the owners of a "Space Conquerors" iPhone will have the choice to select which famous space explorer they would like to see on their device.

Related: 7 Shockingly Expensive Cell Phone Cases

Caviar's designs will include an iconic lineup with Yuri Gagarin, Neil Armstrong, Sergei Korolev, Jeff Bezos and Elon Musk. These fancy creations all make for very unique pieces of art.

Needless to say that these smartphones come at a hefty cost, and therefore will interest buyers who value their device's aesthetic over their bare functions. As a matter of fact, the price of the "Space Conquerors" phones starts at $5,840

The expensive price tag is justified by the costly materials used. The iPhone 12 Pro Max Gagarin edition, for instance, is crafted out of aviation-grade titanium and has a 3D miniature-sized Vostok-K rocket. This is a special little addition, as it is the same rocket that brought the Soviet cosmonaut, Yuri Gagarin, to orbit.

For those passionate of space history and science, they will appreciate the fact that the watch is the same one Gagarin used to track his 108 minutes in space. What makes the device all the more unique is the piece of the Vostok-1 capsule embedded in the phone.

The Gagarin model's price starts at $8,920, making it the most expensive device of the collection. The "cheaper" design features a back made out of hardened titanium and depicts a white engraved feature on a blue background that resembles the logo of Blue Origin, which is Jeff Bezos private company, known for developing space rockets.

Each models are limited, with only a dozens of units made.
