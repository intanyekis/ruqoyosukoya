Who is Juju Smith Schuster?

John Sherman Smith-Schuster is an American Football wide receiver. He plays for American National Football League “Kansas City Chiefs”. He also played for the University of Southern California during his college days.

In his college career, has was rated very highly by the experts, and it didn’t take too long for him to draw attention from the recruiters for the top teams.

He is also well known for his social media presence and affinity for esports. Juju was ranked as one of the most marketable players for the NFL in 2019.

Juju Smith Schuster: Age, Parents, Siblings, Family, Ethnicity, Nationality

Juju Smith was born on November 22, 1996, in Long Beach, California. His mother’s name is Sammy Schuster and her sister’s name is So’omalo Schuster. He is of Mixed(African-American and Samoan) ethnicity.

They are raised by their mother as their father left the family when Smith was a kid, and later his stepfather, Lawrence Smith adopted him.

His stepfather has made a huge difference in his life. Smith officially added his stepfather’s surname to his name after he turned 18 in honor of his stepfather.

His aunt started calling Smith ‘John-John’ when he was kind and later nick-named him ‘JuJu’, which he embraced wholeheartedly. He belongs to American Nationality.

Juju Smith Schuster: Education, School/College University

JuJu Smith-Schuster went to Long Beach Polytechnic High School In Long Beach. He used to play for Long Beach Polytechnic since 8 years old. After completing his schooling he joined the University of Southern California in 2014. He played for 2 years for the University of Southern California from 2014 to 2016.

Juju Smith Schuster: Professional Life, Career

JuJu Smith Schuster was very passionate about football since his childhood. He started playing Football at 8 years old in his school life. Later he played for the team of the University of Southern California from the year 2014-2016, in his career game he had four receptions for 123 yards against Fresno State.

Later In 2017, he joined the NFL draft where he was invited to the NFL combine where he performed various drills and also worked privately with Dallas Cowboys. Dallas Cowboys is the only team to arrange a private workout with him as they saw a great future footballer in him. He played in 13 games with 914 receiving yards and 10 touchdowns.

In the second round of the NFL draft, Pittsburgh Steelers recruited Smith as the 62nd player. The club offered him a four-year contract worth $4.19 million also with a signing-on bonus of $1.19 million and a guaranteed $1.84 in his salary. From thereon, Smith joined the Steelers as a sixth wide receiver in their squad.

The squad also had to store players such as Eli Rogers, Justin Hunter, Darrius Heyward-Bey, Antonio Brown, and MArtavis Bryant ahead.
