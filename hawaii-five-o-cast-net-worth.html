Fun Facts About “Hawaii Five-0”

“Hawaii Five-0” is not just a captivating crime drama; it also has some fascinating trivia that fans might not be aware of. Let’s delve into some amusing and intriguing tidbits about the show.

The Theme Song

Did you know that the iconic theme song of “Hawaii Five-0” was originally composed for another TV series? The unforgettable tune was first written by Morton Stevens as the theme for the 1960s detective show “Checkmate.” However, when Leonard Freeman, the producer of “Hawaii Five-0,” heard the music, he immediately fell in love with it and decided to use it for his own show. The rest, as they say, is history.

Legendary Catchphrase

One of the most recognizable catchphrases in television history originated from “Hawaii Five-0.” The iconic line, “Book ’em, Danno,” became synonymous with the show. Interestingly, it was coined by the show’s producer, Leonard Freeman, as a line for Jack Lord’s character, Steve McGarrett, to say to James MacArthur’s character, Dan Williams. This catchy phrase has cemented its place in pop culture and remains a beloved part of the show’s legacy.

The Original Series

The original “Hawaii Five-0” series aired from 1968 to 1980 and had a remarkable run of 12 seasons. Set in present-day Hawaii, the show became a favorite among viewers. With its stunning island backdrop and compelling stories, “Hawaii Five-0” captured the imagination of audiences and left an indelible mark on the television landscape.

Daniel Dae Kim’s Departure from “Hawaii Five-0”

In 2017, actor Daniel Dae Kim made headlines when he left the hit TV show “Hawaii Five-0” after a salary dispute. Alongside co-star Grace Park, Kim sought salary parity with their white colleagues but was offered a lower pay, which led to their departure from the show.

Throughout his time on “Hawaii Five-0,” Daniel Dae Kim portrayed the character Chin Ho Kelly and became a beloved member of the cast. Despite the salary dispute, Kim expressed gratitude for his experience on the show and the opportunity it provided him.
